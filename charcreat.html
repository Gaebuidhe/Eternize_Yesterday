Character creation is meant to develop functioning character within the scope of the setting. Below outlines the basic process to creating a character in simple terms and instructions. It should also be noted, unless otherwise mentioned by the Game Master (GM), that stories focus on themes of exploration and unknowns of the universe at large. Will you play a Jotun Mercenary, an Alfar Scholar, or become the specialized Arcane Engineer?

It should be noted and understood that players are in a group environment. Players should not feel hampered in the character creation process, but your characters will be involved with a party for a marginal amount of time, hopefully. Respect one another above table, and have fun its just a game. If one character has issue with another, that should not leave the in character aspect and boil into out of character conversation.

Character Sheets or regular paper are suggested for use in recording your character.

1. Choose Race

There are a number of races that inhabit the Universe of Eternize Yesterday and playable characters will typically belong to one of these six. Use their attributes for the base of which your character is created.
Above it was mentioned that characters are typically of the following six races, if you have an idea for a race confer with your GM first.
⁍ Alfar
⁍ Aos sí
⁍ Dwarf
⁍ Human
⁍ Jotun
⁍ Leprechaun

Races provides a brief look at all the races available.

2. Choose Class

A character's class represents his particular training or specializations. They give characters attributes and and skill proficiencies.
This is not an all inclusive list of potential class avenues. If you feel there needs to be another class, work with the GM to create one that fits your character motif
⁍ Arcane Engineer
⁍ Diplomat
⁍ Explorer
⁍ Healer
⁍ Mage
⁍ Mercenary
⁍ Scholar
⁍ Vagabond

Classes provides a brief look at all the classes available.

3. Select Additional Skill

Players may select one additional skill of their choice to level. This represents the characters hobbies or personal drives to learn something outside of their class skills.

4. Calculate Proficiences

With all Skill Proficiency Ranks assigned for Character Creation, calculate Proficiency by adding Skill Proficiency Ranks with the corresponding Attribute.
Hit Points are generated by adding Endurance + 13.
Mana Points are Intellect + 8.

5. Pick Two Starting Class Abilities

Now the Players delve into what their characters are particularly good at doing. These are particular abilities that each class typically teaches, but its on the player to pick for their character. Things like throwing weapons, modifying magitechnology, or even blocking for an ally are represented in the class abilities. At character creation each player will pick two of the starting abilities to further flesh out their character. Each level after they will pick an additional ability and at level five they will have the option to begin taking advanced abilities.

Abilities provides descriptions on each of the Class Abilities.
Classes Shows charts for each of the classes and their abilities.

6. Buy Starting Gear

Players will now go into purchasing gear for their character. Characters start with 400 sil to purchase gear and may not purchase an item beyond rarity 7 at character creation. After all gear is purchased and added to the character sheet, any excess sil is added to 50 sil and counts as the players starting funds

Further Developing the Character

It's also important to develop the backstory of a character past the plain statistics. After all people are more than just numbers and figures. Below are some suggested questions to consider while making a character to develop their personality and history:

1. Is this character Adventurous? Why or Why not?
2. Who are this character's parants? Whereare they now?
3. What are this character's immediate goals?
4. Why are they with the party?
5. What is this character's greatest achievment
6. Does this character show mercy?
